What Are My Options For CANADA IMMIGRATION?

There are different reasons a person might want to move to Canada, backed by the various immigration programs Canada offers. Many people desire to immigrate to Canada to study. Others join family members already living in the country or want to work or start a business. At Agate Immigration Solutions, we help immigrants with each of these immigration options.

If you plan to move to Canada, you are about to make a great choice. The land of the maple leaf has got to offer you stunning landscapes, an excellent healthcare system, and an education system that is ranked among the best in the world. The yearly immigration rate in Canada is one of the highest in the world, accommodating approximately 300,000 new immigrants every year. There are several Canadian work permits available for eligible individuals.

All immigration applications can be complicated as it involves research and a lot of time. The initial process needs individuals to submit supporting documents, finish application forms, and convince immigration authorities that they are suitable for their chosen route. Working with an immigration consultant is not essential, but many of our clients have found that doing so can deliver significant support and accurate legal advice.

Speak to Agate Immigration Solution today if you want to work with a qualified immigration adviser on your move to Canada.

  • QUEBEC SKILLED WORKER PROGRAM

    The region of Quebec, under the Canada-Quebec Accord on Immigration, sets up their own choice standards and necessities based on who will adjust well to living in Quebec. In case you're looking to move to the region of Quebec as a talented specialist you should meet their prerequisites, not those of the Federal Skilled Worker Program.

  • QUEBEC EXPERIENCE CLASS

    In the event that you have studied or worked in the province of Quebec, you may be eligible to apply for immigration to Quebec under one of the two categories of the Quebec Experience Class (Programme de l’expérience Québécoise, or PEQ). Foreign Students who received a degree from a Quebec institution within three years or are in the process of getting one may apply for permanent residence in Quebec under the PEQ. Temporary Foreign Workers may be eligible to apply for this program if they currently hold a job in Quebec, have held a job for at least one year in the last two years, and are proficient in French.
